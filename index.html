Overview

AI-based generative technologies are now a professional-grade production tool for filmmaking. How these systems are shaped will be determined by the communities engaging most directly with them. We believe they should be shaped by the artists and filmmakers most impacted by their integration into professional environments.

We work collaboratively with directors, producers, cinematographers, and artists to develop custom generative systems for specific projects — aggressively asserting the value of the collaborative, deeply authored processes that filmmakers have refined over more than a century. Our mission is to advocate for a world where these tools enable economic responsibility and enhanced creative expression, not replacement and compromise.

Pre-Production

Capabilities Overview

We preview the possibilities and help you develop a production plan.

Before a single frame is shot, we sit down with your team — director, DP, line producer, production designer — and demonstrate what generative workflows can do for your specific project. We map capabilities to your budget gaps and creative ambitions, showing you where AI creates real value and where it doesn't belong.

Relighting:

Expand your lighting options without expanding your lighting budget.

Generative relighting lets you explore lighting setups that weren't achievable on the day — golden hour you missed, a motivated interior source you couldn't rig, a night exterior that would have required a full grip package. The image stays grounded in your footage. The light changes.

Style Transfer:

Explore locations and production design that weren't budgetarily feasible.

Style transfer maps the visual grammar of one world onto another. A contemporary apartment becomes a period interior. A studio backlot reads as a foreign location. This isn't about replacing your production designer — it's about giving them a broader canvas when the budget says no.

Alternative Angles:

Get the missing shot. Finish the day before overtime.

Every production has the shot they didn't get — the reverse that would complete the scene, the wide that establishes geography, the insert that sells the beat. Generative angle synthesis gives you coverage options from footage you already have, reducing the pressure on shooting days and cutting overtime costs.

Outpainting & Background Removal:

Change the frame. Extend the world.

Outpainting expands your frame beyond what the lens captured — wider compositions, adjusted aspect ratios, elements that extend the production design past the edge of set. Background Removal isolates your subject cleanly, opening up compositing possibilities that would otherwise require expensive greenscreen setups.

Alt Wardrobe & Production Design:

Cut your wardrobe and production design budgets without cutting your vision.

Explore costume variations, set dressing options, and production design directions at the speed of iteration, not fabrication. Use generative tools to test creative directions before committing physical resources — then only build what you've already validated visually.

Production

Ingestion + Refinement

We finetune models and develop bespoke workflows using your production assets during principal photography.

During production, we ingest your dailies, set designs, location photography, wardrobe references, and motion capture data. This material becomes the foundation for custom models — LoRAs, embeddings, and tailored workflows — that learn the specific visual language of your project.

The result: by the time you wrap, your post-production toolkit isn't generic. It's trained on your own data, eliminating copyright concerns.

Ingestion + Refinement: Process

From unstructured production data to production-ready AI systems.

Your proprietary assets — dailies, stills, production design materials, motion reference — are processed into structured formats: LoRA models trained on your visual identity, CLIP embeddings mapped to your aesthetic space, and custom ComfyUI workflows calibrated to your project's specific needs.

We create a custom tuned generative system that automatically generates video in the existing visual language of the film including subject, context and aesthetics.

LoRA Examples

Finetuning is the difference between a generative tool and your generative tool.

LoRA training adapts foundation models to the specific visual identity of your project — your actor's likeness, your production design palette, your cinematographer's lighting sensibility. The result is generative output that lives inside your film's world, not adjacent to it.
